Logo do's

  • Keep generous padding around the lockup — at least the height of the FE emblem.
  • Use the dark-background lockup on photography and dark hero sections.
  • Use the light-background lockup on white or cream surfaces.
  • Pair the emblem alone for square avatars (Facebook, Google profile).

Logo don'ts

  • Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the lockup.
  • Don't recolor the FE emblem outside the approved palette.
  • Don't place the wordmark on busy photos without a dark overlay.
  • Don't substitute the typeface — Oswald only for the wordmark.

05 · Voice & Tone

Confident. Local. Direct.

Write like Joe is standing in your kitchen — not like a corporate brand. Short sentences. Real words. No hype. The reader should feel like they're hiring a real person who shows up and does the job right.

Do this

  • Write in plain English a homeowner uses out loud.
  • Lead with what the visitor gets — safety, peace of mind, a flat-rate quote.
  • Use proof in the headline: license, jobs completed, "same-day."
  • Speak to fear honestly: flickering lights, warm outlets, sparking.
  • Close with a clear next step: call, text, or get a quote.
  • Reference Colorado Springs and the Front Range often.

Not this

  • "Synergize", "leverage", "best-in-class", or any corporate filler.
  • Generic "quality work, fair prices" without proof points behind it.
  • Long, run-on sentences that feel like a brochure.
  • Hype, exclamation marks, or fake urgency ("ACT NOW!!!").
  • Talking about ourselves before talking about the visitor.
  • Vague claims ("trusted nationwide") that don't fit a local brand.
